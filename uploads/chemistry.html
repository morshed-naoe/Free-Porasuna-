Oxidation and Reduction (Redox)

Chemical Reaction Example:

2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO

Explanation:

This is a classic Redox reaction where both oxidation and reduction happen at the same time.

1. Oxidation (জারণ):
Magnesium (Mg) loses electrons to become Mg²⁺. Loss of electrons is called oxidation.
Reaction: Mg → Mg²⁺ + 2e⁻
2. Reduction (বিজারণ):
Oxygen (O₂) gains electrons to become O²⁻. Gain of electrons is called reduction.
Reaction: O₂ + 4e⁻ → 2O²⁻

Conclusion: In this reaction, Magnesium is oxidized and Oxygen is reduced. Together they form Magnesium Oxide (MgO).
